# Utility Theory

A formal calculus for *preferences* under uncertainty. Rational decision making maximizes expected utility.

## Foundations
**Von Neumann–Morgenstern** axioms (completeness, transitivity, continuity, independence) → utility function U exists s.t. preferences are captured by E[U].

## Key Concepts
- **Risk attitudes:** concave U → risk averse, linear → risk neutral, convex → risk seeking
- **Certainty equivalent:** the certain payoff equally preferred to a lottery
- **Utility of money** is typically concave (diminishing marginal utility)
- **Multi-attribute utility** for decisions with multiple objectives
- **Prospect theory** — descriptive corrections (loss aversion, probability weighting)
- **Irrationality** — observed deviations from EU theory

## Connection to ML
- [[decision-theory]] uses utility / loss interchangeably (loss = -utility)
- Preference learning, utility elicitation drive recommender systems and RLHF
